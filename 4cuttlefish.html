The "cuttle" in cuttlefish comes from the Old English name for the species, which may be cognate with the Old Norse koddi (cushion) and the.[6] The Greco-Roman world valued the cuttlefish as a source of the unique brown pigment the creature releases from its siphon when it is alarmed. The word for it in both Greek and Latin, sepia, now refers to the reddish-brown color sepia in English.
